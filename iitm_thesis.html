Dual Degree Thesis Project, IIT Madras

Researcher, Jun 2022 - May 2023

As part of my thesis project at IIT Madras, I worked on the development of Reinforcement Learning based fast charging algorithms for Lithium ion cells along with my advisor Prof. R Krishna Kumar. I chose to work on this problem as it felt like a natural continuation from my internship, and it would allow me to apply the learnings from past projects and coursework to a problem in the EV domain.

To start, I built a detailed model of a LiFePO₄ cell using MATLAB Simulink, incorporating its electrical, thermal, and aging characteristics. This model served as the testbed for my algorithms, and to compare it against other charging techniques. I utilised the model-free Proximal Policy Optimisation approach to train the RL agent. The state of the cell was defined using 6 parameters, capturing the state of charge, health, temperature, and input current, while the reward function was designed to minimize charging time within safe temperature and current limits.

Results showed that the developed algorithm could achieve charging twice as fast as the conventional constant current - constant voltage (CCCV) method at comparable ambient conditions, while maintaining similar levels of battery degradation. Additionally, the algorithm was capable of fast charging within 20 minutes at temperatures as high as 40℃, while maintaining nominal battery degradation. Ultimately, the abstract was selected for the Cyber-Physical Systems Summit 2023 at IIT Kharagpur.
